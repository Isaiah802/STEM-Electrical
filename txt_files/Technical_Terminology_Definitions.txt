   Fixed-Wing Environmental Monitoring UAV
   NLC STEM Club - Electrical Systems Team

DOCUMENT PURPOSE:
  This glossary defines all technical terms, acronyms, and concepts used throughout
  the UAV electrical system documentation. Use this as a quick reference guide when
  encountering unfamiliar terminology.

ORGANIZATION:
  Terms are organized alphabetically within categories for easy lookup.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   SECTION 1: ELECTRICAL TERMS & CONCEPTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

AMPERE (A) / MILLIAMPERE (mA)
  Unit of electrical current. 1 Ampere = 1000 milliamperes.
  Example: The flight controller draws 250mA (0.25A) of current.

AWG (American Wire Gauge)
  Standard system for measuring wire thickness. LOWER numbers = THICKER wire.
  - 12 AWG: Very thick, high current (30A+) - motor wires
  - 18 AWG: Medium, moderate current (5-10A) - power distribution
  - 26 AWG: Thin, low current/signal (<1A) - sensor wires
  The thicker the wire, the less resistance and more current it can safely carry.

BACKFEEDING
  Unwanted flow of power in the reverse direction through a circuit. In our system,
  this occurs if the ESC's +5V wire is connected to the FC, potentially damaging
  the FC's voltage regulator. SOLUTION: Cut the middle wire on ESC signal cable.

BEC (Battery Eliminator Circuit)
  A voltage regulator that converts high battery voltage (14.8V) down to 5V for
  electronics. "Eliminates" the need for a separate battery for radio equipment.
  Our ESC has a built-in 5V 3A BEC (Linear BEC - less efficient but simpler).

BROWNOUT
  Temporary voltage drop that causes electronics to malfunction or reset. Occurs
  when too much current is drawn from a power source, causing voltage to sag below
  minimum operating level. Our Dual-BEC design prevents FC brownout from servo loads.

CAPACITY (mAh or Ah)
  Milliamp-hours or Amp-hours. Measures how much charge a battery can store.
  - Our battery: 2200mAh = can deliver 2200mA for 1 hour (or 1100mA for 2 hours)
  - Higher capacity = longer flight time (but also heavier battery)

CONTINUITY
  Unbroken electrical path. Used in testing: "check continuity" means verify a
  wire is not broken and conducts electricity. Measured with multimeter in Ω mode.

CURRENT (I)
  Flow of electrical charge, measured in Amperes (A). Like water flow in a pipe.
  Formula: I = V / R (Current = Voltage / Resistance)
  - Typical current: FC draws 250mA
  - Peak current: Motor can draw 30A during full throttle

DUAL-BEC CONFIGURATION
  Our chosen power architecture using TWO separate voltage regulators:
  - BEC 1 (ESC integrated): Powers flight-critical systems (FC, GPS, RX)
  - BEC 2 (External UBEC): Powers high-current servos
  Advantage: Isolates servo power spikes from flight controller, preventing crashes.

DUTY CYCLE
  Percentage of time a signal is "ON" vs "OFF". Used in PWM control.
  - 50% duty cycle = signal is high 50% of the time
  - Servo control: 1ms pulse (5% duty cycle) = full left, 2ms (10%) = full right

ELECTRICAL NOISE / EMI (Electromagnetic Interference)
  Unwanted electrical signals that interfere with sensitive electronics. Sources:
  - Motor/ESC switching (high-frequency noise)
  - Power supply ripple
  - Radio frequency interference
  Mitigation: Twisted wires, distance separation, filtering capacitors.

GND (Ground)
  Reference point for voltage measurement, typically 0V. Also completes circuit
  for current flow. CRITICAL: All components must share a common ground or signals
  will be corrupted. Our design uses star grounding topology.

LOAD
  Any device that consumes electrical power. In our system:
  - Light load: GPS (60mA)
  - Medium load: Arduino (263mA)
  - Heavy load: Motor (10-30A)

OHMS LAW
  Fundamental relationship: V = I × R (Voltage = Current × Resistance)
  Also: I = V / R and R = V / I
  Used to calculate current draw, voltage drop, wire sizing, etc.

PARALLEL CONNECTION
  Components connected with all positive terminals together and all negative
  terminals together. Voltage stays same, currents add.
  Example: All 4 servos powered in parallel from UBEC (each gets 5V, currents sum).

POLARITY
  Direction of electrical flow. Positive (+) and Negative (-) terminals.
  ⚠ CRITICAL: Reversed polarity can instantly destroy electronics!
  Always verify RED = positive, BLACK = negative before connecting.

POWER (W, Watts)
  Rate of energy consumption. Formula: P = V × I (Power = Voltage × Current)
  Example: Motor at 14.8V drawing 20A = 296W power consumption
  Our battery: 2200mAh × 14.8V = 32.56Wh (Watt-hours) total energy

POWER RAIL
  Distribution bus that supplies power to multiple components at same voltage.
  Our system has two 5V rails:
  - Rail 1: Flight-critical (FC, GPS, RX, Arduino) - ESC BEC
  - Rail 2: Servo power (all 4 servos) - External UBEC

RESISTANCE (R)
  Opposition to current flow, measured in Ohms (Ω). Like friction in a pipe.
  - Low resistance: Thick wires, good connections
  - High resistance: Thin wires, corroded connections
  Causes voltage drop and heat generation.

SERIES CONNECTION
  Components connected end-to-end so current flows through each in sequence.
  Voltages add, current stays same.
  Example: Battery cells in series (3.7V + 3.7V + 3.7V + 3.7V = 14.8V for 4S).

SHORT CIRCUIT
  Unintended direct connection between positive and negative, bypassing load.
  Results in VERY high current, heat, fire risk, component damage.
  ⚠ ALWAYS use fuse/protection and inspect for bare wire touching!

STAR GROUNDING
  Grounding topology where all ground wires connect to ONE central point (battery
  negative) rather than daisy-chaining. Prevents ground loops and voltage differences
  between components. REQUIRED for our system's proper operation.

UBEC (Universal Battery Eliminator Circuit)
  Advanced switching voltage regulator (vs linear BEC). More efficient, handles
  higher input voltage range, generates less heat. Our external UBEC is 5V 6A
  switching regulator for servo power.

VOLTAGE (V)
  Electrical potential difference, measured in Volts. Like water pressure in a pipe.
  - Battery voltage: 14.8V nominal (13.2-16.8V range for 4S LiPo)
  - Regulated voltage: 5.0V for electronics
  - Signal voltage: 3.3V or 5V logic levels

VOLTAGE DROP
  Reduction in voltage due to resistance in wires/connections. Formula: V_drop = I × R
  Problem: Long thin wires carrying high current have significant voltage drop.
  Solution: Use appropriately thick wire for current level (AWG sizing).

VOLTAGE SAG
  Temporary voltage drop when high current is suddenly drawn. Battery internal
  resistance causes voltage to "sag" under heavy load. Can cause brownout if
  excessive. Our high C-rating battery (130C) minimizes voltage sag.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   SECTION 2: COMPONENT TERMINOLOGY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARDUINO
  Open-source microcontroller platform. Our Arduino Nano uses ATmega328P chip
  (8-bit, 16MHz, 32KB flash) for data logging and sensor interfacing. Programmed
  in C/C++ using Arduino IDE.

BAROMETER
  Atmospheric pressure sensor (BMP280 on flight controller). Measures air pressure
  to calculate altitude. Used for altitude hold and vertical navigation.

BLDC MOTOR (Brushless DC Motor)
  Electric motor with electronic commutation (no brushes). More efficient, reliable,
  and powerful than brushed motors. Requires ESC for speed control. Our D2830EVO
  is a 3-phase outrunner BLDC motor.

C-RATING (Battery)
  Discharge rate capability of LiPo battery. Formula: Max Current = Capacity × C-Rating
  Our battery: 2.2Ah × 130C = 286A maximum burst current (never approach this!)
  Higher C-rating = lower internal resistance = less voltage sag under load.

COMPASS (Magnetometer)
  Sensor that detects Earth's magnetic field for heading determination. Integrated
  in BN-220 GPS module (HMC5883L chip). Requires calibration and distance from
  magnetic interference (motors, current-carrying wires).

ESC (Electronic Speed Controller)
  Power electronics that control brushless motor speed. Converts DC battery power
  to 3-phase AC for motor. Receives PWM throttle signal from FC, outputs variable
  frequency/amplitude to motor. Our 40A ESC has integrated 5V BEC.

FC (Flight Controller)
  Central computer that stabilizes aircraft and executes flight commands. Our
  MATEK F405-WMN has:
  - STM32F405 processor (32-bit ARM, 168MHz)
  - IMU (gyro + accelerometer) for attitude sensing
  - Runs iNav or ArduPilot firmware

FOLDING PROPELLER
  Propeller with blades that fold back when motor is off (reduces drag in glide).
  Spring-loaded to deploy under centrifugal force when spinning. Used on our
  motor for efficient glide performance.

GPS (Global Positioning System)
  Satellite navigation system. Our BN-220 module receives signals from GPS
  satellites to determine 3D position (latitude, longitude, altitude) and velocity.
  Requires clear view of sky; 6+ satellites for good fix.

GYROSCOPE
  Sensor that measures angular rotation rate (degrees per second). Part of IMU
  on flight controller. Used for stabilization - detects when aircraft is rotating
  and commands control surfaces to counteract.

HEAT SHRINK TUBING
  Plastic tubing that shrinks when heated, used to insulate electrical connections.
  Always cover solder joints and bare wire with heat shrink to prevent shorts.

IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit)
  Combined sensor package: 3-axis gyroscope + 3-axis accelerometer = 6 DOF
  (degrees of freedom). Measures rotation and acceleration in all directions.
  Flight controller uses IMU data for stabilization and attitude estimation.

JST CONNECTOR
  Small standardized connector series. Common types:
  - JST-XH: Battery balance connector
  - JST-SH: 1.0mm pitch (GPS, receiver)
  - JST-PH: 2.0mm pitch (general electronics)

KV RATING (Motor)
  RPM per volt constant. Our 1000KV motor spins at 1000 RPM for every volt applied.
  At 14.8V: 14,800 RPM (no load)
  Lower KV = more torque, larger props; Higher KV = less torque, smaller props.

LiPo (Lithium Polymer Battery)
  Rechargeable battery with high energy density. Chemistry: Lithium-ion with
  polymer electrolyte. Nominal voltage: 3.7V per cell.
  - 1S = 3.7V, 2S = 7.4V, 3S = 11.1V, 4S = 14.8V (our battery)
  ⚠ DANGEROUS: Can catch fire if damaged, overcharged, or short-circuited!

OSD (On-Screen Display)
  Overlays flight telemetry on FPV video feed. MATEK F405-WMN has integrated OSD
  chip that can display battery voltage, current, altitude, GPS data, etc. on
  screen. Configured in iNav/ArduPilot.

OUTRUNNER MOTOR
  BLDC motor where outer case rotates and center is stationary (opposite of
  inrunner). Higher torque, better for direct-drive propellers. Our D2830EVO is
  an outrunner.

RECEIVER (RX)
  Radio receiver that picks up transmitter signals. Our HAPPYMODEL EP2 receives
  ExpressLRS 2.4GHz radio commands and sends them to FC via CRSF or SBUS protocol.

SENSOR FUSION
  Algorithm that combines data from multiple sensors (GPS, IMU, barometer, compass)
  to get better position/attitude estimate than any single sensor. Flight controller
  firmware does this automatically.

SERVO
  Electromechanical actuator with position feedback. Receives PWM pulse (1-2ms)
  and rotates output shaft to corresponding angle. Our ES08MA II servos have:
  - Metal gears (durable)
  - 180° rotation
  - Proportional control

TRANSMITTER (TX)
  Hand-held radio controller. Pilot inputs (sticks, switches) converted to radio
  signals and sent to receiver on aircraft. Can also refer to data transmit pin
  on serial connection.

UBEC (Previously Defined)
  See Section 1. External voltage regulator providing 5V @ 6A for servo power.

XT60 CONNECTOR
  High-current connector rated for 60A continuous. Yellow plastic housing with
  spring-pin contacts. Standard for 3S-6S LiPo batteries. Gender: Male on battery,
  Female on ESC/cables.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   SECTION 3: PROTOCOLS & COMMUNICATION STANDARDS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ANALOG SIGNAL
  Continuously variable voltage representing data. Our MQ135 gas sensor outputs
  0-5V analog voltage proportional to gas concentration. Read by Arduino ADC
  (Analog-to-Digital Converter).

BAUD RATE
  Speed of serial communication in bits per second (bps). Common rates:
  - 9600 baud: GPS default (slow but reliable)
  - 115200 baud: Fast serial (firmware upload, debugging)
  - 420000 baud: CRSF protocol from ELRS receiver
  Both devices must use SAME baud rate to communicate.

CRSF (Crossfire Protocol)
  Bi-directional serial protocol for RC control and telemetry. Developed by TBS,
  now used by ExpressLRS. 420000 baud, low latency, supports up to 16 channels
  plus telemetry data. Our ELRS receiver uses CRSF to talk to FC.

DIGITAL SIGNAL
  Discrete on/off voltage levels representing binary data (0 or 1).
  - Logic LOW: 0V (binary 0)
  - Logic HIGH: 3.3V or 5V (binary 1)
  Used for: I2C, SPI, UART, PWM control signals.

ELRS (ExpressLRS)
  Open-source long-range radio control system using 2.4GHz or 900MHz. Features:
  - Very low latency (5-20ms)
  - Long range (10+ km)
  - Frequency hopping for interference immunity
  - Over-the-air updates
  Our EP2 receiver uses ELRS protocol.

I2C (Inter-Integrated Circuit)
  Two-wire serial protocol for short-distance communication. Uses SDA (data) and
  SCL (clock) lines. Multiple devices on same bus (each has unique address).
  Our BME280 sensor uses I2C to send temperature/humidity/pressure data to Arduino.

NMEA (National Marine Electronics Association)
  Text-based protocol for GPS data. Human-readable sentences like:
  $GPGGA,123519,4807.038,N,01131.000,E,1,08,0.9,545.4,M,46.9,M,,*47
  Contains position, time, satellites, etc. Alternative to binary UBX protocol.

PWM (Pulse Width Modulation)
  Signal where information is encoded in pulse duration. Used for:
  - Servo control: 1ms = -90°, 1.5ms = center, 2ms = +90°
  - Motor throttle: 1ms = 0%, 2ms = 100%
  - Frequency: typically 50Hz (20ms period) for servos
  Analog value represented by digital signal duty cycle.

SBUS (Serial Bus)
  Inverted serial protocol from FrSky. 100000 baud, 25ms update, 16 channels.
  Less efficient than CRSF but widely compatible. Fallback option if CRSF doesn't
  work with our FC firmware.

SPI (Serial Peripheral Interface)
  Four-wire serial protocol: MOSI (master out), MISO (master in), SCK (clock),
  CS (chip select). Fast, full-duplex communication. Our MicroSD card module uses
  SPI to transfer data at high speed from Arduino.

TELEMETRY
  Real-time transmission of aircraft data back to ground station. Can include:
  battery voltage, current, altitude, GPS position, speed, RSSI (signal strength).
  CRSF protocol supports bi-directional telemetry.

UART (Universal Asynchronous Receiver-Transmitter)
  Serial communication protocol using TX (transmit) and RX (receive) wires.
  Asynchronous = no clock signal, must agree on baud rate.
  - UART1 on FC: Connected to GPS (9600 baud)
  - UART2 on FC: Connected to receiver (420000 baud)

UBX PROTOCOL
  Binary GPS protocol from u-blox. More efficient than NMEA, higher update rate,
  configurable messages. Our BN-220 GPS supports both UBX and NMEA.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   SECTION 4: AVIATION & UAV TERMINOLOGY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

AILERON
  Control surface on wing trailing edge that controls roll (banking). Differential
  movement: left aileron up + right aileron down = roll left.
  Our aircraft has 2 ailerons (Servo 1 and Servo 2).

AUTONOMOUS FLIGHT
  Aircraft flies pre-programmed mission without pilot input. Uses GPS waypoints,
  altitude commands, loiter circles. FC firmware executes mission autonomously.
  Requires GPS lock and properly tuned flight controller.

BLACKBOX
  Flight data recorder that logs sensor data at high rate (1000Hz+) for post-
  flight analysis. Can be onboard flash memory or microSD card. Used for tuning
  PIDs, diagnosing crashes, analyzing performance.

CONTROL SURFACE
  Movable aerodynamic surface that controls aircraft attitude:
  - Ailerons: Roll control
  - Elevator: Pitch control
  - Rudder: Yaw control

ELEVATOR
  Control surface on horizontal tail that controls pitch (nose up/down).
  Our Servo 3 controls the elevator.

ELRS (Already Defined)
  See Section 3 - ExpressLRS long-range control system.

FAILSAFE
  Pre-programmed behavior when radio link is lost. Options:
  - Hold position (loiter)
  - Return to home (RTH) and land
  - Immediate land
  - Cut throttle (glide down)
  Configured in flight controller firmware.

FIXED-WING
  Aircraft with continuous rigid wings (like airplane), as opposed to rotorcraft
  (quadcopter). Our UAV is fixed-wing design. Requires forward motion for lift;
  can glide efficiently but cannot hover.

FPV (First Person View)
  Flying from onboard camera perspective. Video transmitted to pilot's goggles/
  screen in real-time. Our project focuses on autonomous flight, not FPV, but
  FC has OSD capability for future FPV addition.

GPS LOCK / FIX
  Successful acquisition of GPS satellite signals allowing position determination.
  Requires:
  - Clear sky view
  - 4+ satellites for 2D fix (lat/long)
  - 6+ satellites for 3D fix (lat/long/altitude)
  Indicated by LED on GPS module.

IMU (Already Defined)
  See Section 2 - Inertial Measurement Unit for stabilization.

LOITER / POSITION HOLD
  Flight mode where aircraft maintains fixed GPS position (circles within radius).
  Requires GPS lock. Useful for observing area or waiting for telemetry.

PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative)
  Control algorithm used for stabilization. FC continuously adjusts control surfaces
  to maintain desired attitude:
  - P: Correction proportional to error (immediate response)
  - I: Correction based on accumulated error (eliminates steady-state error)
  - D: Correction based on rate of error change (dampening)
  Tuning PID values is critical for stable flight.

PITCH
  Rotation about lateral axis (nose up/down). Controlled by elevator.

PROPELLER
  Rotating blade that converts motor power to thrust. Specifications:
  - Diameter × Pitch: 1060 = 10" diameter, 6" pitch
  - Pitch = distance propeller advances in one rotation
  - Folding prop: Blades fold back when not spinning

ROLL
  Rotation about longitudinal axis (banking left/right). Controlled by ailerons.

RTH (Return to Home)
  Autonomous flight mode where aircraft flies back to takeoff location using GPS.
  Can be triggered by pilot command, failsafe, or low battery.

RUDDER
  Control surface on vertical tail that controls yaw (nose left/right).
  Our Servo 4 controls the rudder. Less critical in calm wind than ailerons/elevator.

STABILIZATION
  Automatic correction to maintain level flight. Gyros detect rotation, FC commands
  control surfaces to counteract. Modes:
  - Acro: No stabilization (manual input only)
  - Angle: Self-leveling (returns to level when sticks centered)
  - Horizon: mix of Acro and Angle

UAV (Unmanned Aerial Vehicle)
  Aircraft without human pilot onboard. Controlled remotely or autonomously.
  Synonyms: drone, UAS (Unmanned Aircraft System), remotely piloted aircraft.

YAW
  Rotation about vertical axis (nose left/right). Controlled by rudder.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   SECTION 5: SENSOR & MEASUREMENT TERMINOLOGY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ADC (Analog-to-Digital Converter)
  Circuit that converts analog voltage to digital number. Arduino Nano has 10-bit
  ADC (0-1023 range) for reading analog sensors. MQ135 gas sensor output is read
  by ADC on pin A0.

BME280
  Combined environmental sensor IC (Bosch Sensortec):
  - Barometric pressure: 300-1100 hPa (altitude 0-30,000 ft)
  - Temperature: -40 to +85°C (±1°C accuracy)
  - Humidity: 0-100% RH (±3% accuracy)
  Uses I2C interface. Perfect for weather monitoring mission.

BURN-IN PERIOD
  Initial operation time required for sensor to stabilize. MQ135 gas sensor requires
  24-48 hours of continuous power before accurate readings. Tin oxide sensor
  element needs to heat up and stabilize chemically.

CALIBRATION
  Procedure to adjust sensor readings to known reference values. Examples:
  - Compass: Rotate aircraft through 360° to map magnetic field distortions
  - ESC: Set throttle range endpoints (min/max PWM values)
  - Gas sensor: Expose to fresh air and record baseline reading

CSV (Comma-Separated Values)
  Simple text file format for data logging. Each line is one record, values
  separated by commas. Example:
  Time,Temp,Humidity,AirQuality
  0,24.5,65,450
  Our Arduino saves sensor data in CSV format for analysis in Excel/Python.

DATA LOGGING
  Recording sensor measurements over time for post-flight analysis. Our system:
  - Timestamp from millis() function
  - BME280 readings every second
  - MQ135 readings every second
  - Written to microSD card in CSV format

I2C ADDRESS
  Unique 7-bit number identifying device on I2C bus. BME280 default address:
  0x76 or 0x77 (solder jumper selectable). Scanner sketch can find device addresses.

MicroSD CARD
  Small removable flash storage. Our module uses SPI interface for data transfer.
  Format: FAT32 recommended (up to 32GB cards). Stores sensor data logs as CSV files.

MQ135
  Metal oxide gas sensor for air quality monitoring. Detects:
  - CO2 (carbon dioxide)
  - NH3 (ammonia)
  - NOx (nitrogen oxides)
  - Alcohol, smoke, benzene
  Outputs analog voltage (0-5V) proportional to gas concentration. Requires 5V
  supply and 24-48 hour burn-in period. Not highly selective (detects multiple gases).

RESOLUTION
  Smallest change a sensor can detect. Examples:
  - BME280 temperature: 0.01°C resolution
  - Arduino ADC: 5V / 1024 = 4.88mV resolution (10-bit)
  Higher resolution = more precise measurements.

SAMPLING RATE
  Frequency of sensor measurements. Our system: 1 Hz (one reading per second).
  Higher sampling rate = more data, faster battery drain, larger log files.

SENSOR FUSION (Already Defined)
  See Section 3 - Combining multiple sensor inputs for better accuracy.

TIMESTAMP
  Time marker for each data point. Arduino uses millis() function (milliseconds
  since power-on). Format: Unix timestamp or formatted date/time string.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   SECTION 6: SOFTWARE & FIRMWARE TERMINOLOGY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARDUINO IDE
  Software development environment for programming Arduino boards. Uses simplified
  C/C++ language. Includes code editor, compiler, and upload tool. Free and
  open-source.

ArduPilot
  Open-source autopilot software for fixed-wing, multirotor, and other vehicles.
  Features: GPS navigation, autonomous missions, advanced sensors. Alternative
  firmware for our MATEK F405-WMN (in addition to iNav).

BOOTLOADER
  Small program that allows firmware updates via USB. Pre-installed on Arduino
  and flight controllers. Runs first on power-up, checks for upload request.

CONFIGURATOR
  GUI software for flight controller setup. Options:
  - iNav Configurator (for iNav firmware)
  - Mission Planner (for ArduPilot)
  - Betaflight Configurator (for Betaflight)
  Used to configure PID values, modes, calibration, OSD layout.

FIRMWARE
  Low-level software running directly on microcontroller. Read-only memory,
  flashed via USB. Examples:
  - iNav on flight controller
  - Arduino sketch on Arduino Nano
  - ELRS on receiver

FLASHING
  Uploading firmware to microcontroller's flash memory. Process:
  1. Connect device via USB
  2. Select COM port in software
  3. Click "Flash" button
  4. Wait for completion (device reboots automatically)

GITHUB
  Online platform for hosting open-source code. ELRS, iNav, ArduPilot all hosted
  on GitHub. Can download latest versions, report bugs, contribute code.

iNav
  Open-source flight control firmware optimized for fixed-wing and long-range
  flight. Features GPS navigation, missions, return-to-home. Alternative to
  ArduPilot, easier setup. Recommended for our project.

LIBRARY
  Pre-written code that provides functionality for common tasks. Arduino libraries
  for our project:
  - Adafruit_BME280: BME280 sensor interface
  - SD: MicroSD card file operations
  - Wire: I2C communication

MISSION PLANNING
  Creating pre-programmed flight path with GPS waypoints. Software:
  - Mission Planner (ArduPilot)
  - iNav Configurator (iNav)
  Define takeoff, waypoints (lat/long/alt), loiter circles, landing.

OSD LAYOUT
  Configuration of on-screen display elements (position, size, visibility).
  Can show: battery voltage, current, altitude, GPS position, speed, heading,
  artificial horizon, etc.

SKETCH
  Arduino program file (.ino extension). Contains setup() function (runs once)
  and loop() function (runs repeatedly). Our data logging sketch reads sensors
  and writes to SD card.

USB BOOTLOADER (See BOOTLOADER)

WAYPOINT
  GPS coordinate (latitude, longitude, altitude) defining point in mission.
  Aircraft flies from waypoint to waypoint autonomously. Can specify actions
  at each waypoint (loiter time, heading, speed).


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   SECTION 7: SAFETY & TESTING TERMINOLOGY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

BALANCE CHARGING
  LiPo charging method that ensures all cells reach same voltage. Uses balance
  connector (JST-XH) to monitor individual cell voltages. REQUIRED for safe
  LiPo charging. Never charge without balance lead connected!

BENCH TEST
  Testing on workbench before installation in aircraft. Advantages:
  - Safer (no propeller)
  - Easier troubleshooting (access to all components)
  - Can measure voltages and currents
  Always bench test before flight test!

CELL VOLTAGE
  Voltage of individual cell in multi-cell battery. LiPo safe voltages:
  - Full charge: 4.2V per cell
  - Nominal: 3.7V per cell
  - Safe minimum: 3.3V per cell
  - DANGER: 3.0V per cell (damages cell permanently)
  4S battery: 16.8V full, 14.8V nominal, 13.2V minimum

CONTINUITY TEST
  Using multimeter to verify electrical connection (wire not broken). Set meter
  to Ω mode, touch probes to both ends of wire. Should beep/read ~0Ω if continuous.

FAILSAFE TEST
  Verifying aircraft behavior when radio link is lost. Process:
  1. Arm aircraft on ground
  2. Turn off transmitter
  3. Verify failsafe activates (motor cuts, servos go to preset positions)
  4. Turn transmitter back on
  5. Verify control restored

FIRE EXTINGUISHER
  Required safety equipment for LiPo batteries. Class D (metal fire) or ABC
  extinguisher. LiPo fires extremely hot and dangerous. Alternative: Sand bucket
  or metal container to smother fire.

FUSE
  Protective device that breaks circuit if current exceeds rated value. Should
  be installed on battery positive lead. Our system: 40A fuse protects against
  short circuits, prevents battery damage and fire risk.

GROUND TEST
  Final system check before flight:
  - Power on sequence (battery last)
  - GPS lock verification
  - Radio range test (walk 50m away, verify control)
  - Failsafe test
  - Control surface deflection check (full travel, correct direction)

LIPO-SAFE BAG
  Fire-resistant bag for charging and storing LiPo batteries. Contains fire if
  battery catches fire during charging. NOT fireproof (will eventually burn through),
  but provides time to move outside. Alternative: Metal ammo can.

PUFFED BATTERY
  Damaged LiPo battery with swollen cells (bulging). Caused by overcharge, over-
  discharge, physical damage, or age. ⚠ EXTREMELY DANGEROUS - can explode or
  catch fire! Dispose immediately at battery recycling center.

RANGE TEST
  Verifying radio control range before flight. ELRS: Built-in range test mode
  reduces TX power to simulate long distance. Walk away from aircraft until warning
  (should be >100m). Verify control and failsafe work correctly.

SMOKE TEST
  Informal term: First power-on of new build. "If it doesn't smoke, it works!"
  Serious first power-up procedure:
  1. Visual inspection (no shorts, correct polarity)
  2. Connect battery slowly, watch for sparks
  3. Feel components for excessive heat
  4. Measure voltages with multimeter
  5. If anything smokes/smells: DISCONNECT IMMEDIATELY!

STORAGE VOLTAGE
  Safe voltage for long-term LiPo storage: 3.8V per cell (15.2V for 4S).
  Reduces stress on cells, prolongs battery life. Use charger's "storage mode"
  if not flying for >1 week.

THERMAL RUNWAY
  Uncontrolled exothermic reaction in damaged LiPo battery. Cell overheats, causing
  chemical breakdown, generating more heat, causing neighboring cells to fail.
  Results in fire/explosion. Prevention: Proper charging, damage inspection,
  voltage monitoring.

VOLTAGE ALARM
  Small device that beeps when LiPo voltage drops below set threshold. Plugs into
  balance connector. Prevents over-discharge during flight. Set alarm at 3.5V/cell
  (14.0V for 4S) for safety margin.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   SECTION 8: CONNECTORS & MECHANICAL TERMINOLOGY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

BULLET CONNECTOR
  Cylindrical push-fit electrical connector. Common sizes:
  - 2mm: Low current signal wires
  - 3.5mm: Motor phase wires (our ESC to motor)
  - 4mm: High current battery connections
  Male = pin (on wire), Female = socket (on wire or component)

CRIMPING
  Mechanical joining of connector to wire using special tool. Alternative to
  soldering for some connectors (JST, Dupont). Requires proper crimp tool for
  connector size.

DUPONT CONNECTOR
  0.1" pitch plastic housing with crimped pins. Used for breadboard connections,
  Arduino headers, sensor wiring. Can make custom cable lengths. Separate male
  and female pins.

HEAT SHRINK (Already Defined)
  See Section 1 - Insulating tubing that shrinks when heated.

JST CONNECTOR (Already Defined)
  See Section 2 - Small standardized connector family.

MOUNTING TAPE / FOAM TAPE
  Double-sided foam tape for securing electronics. Provides vibration dampening
  and electrical insulation. Use for mounting FC, GPS, receiver.

SERVO ARM / SERVO HORN
  Plastic attachment on servo output shaft. Connects to pushrod/linkage for
  control surface actuation. Usually cross-shaped with multiple holes for different
  throw distances.

SERVO EXTENSION
  Cable to extend servo wire length. Standard 3-pin, male-female. Available in
  various lengths (6", 12", 24"). Allows mounting servos far from FC.

SOLDER / SOLDERING
  Joining metal parts with molten metal alloy (tin-lead or lead-free). Process:
  1. Heat joint with soldering iron (300-350°C)
  2. Apply solder (melts and flows into joint)
  3. Remove iron, let cool (2-3 seconds)
  Good solder joint: Shiny, smooth, no cold spots. Bad joint: Dull, cracked,
  blobby (redo it).

TWIST TIE / ZIP TIE / CABLE TIE
  Plastic strap for bundling and securing wires. Prevents wires from snagging
  on moving parts, keeps electronics neat. Use locking zip ties (not reusable)
  for permanent installation.

WIRE STRIPPING
  Removing insulation from wire end to expose conductor. Use wire strippers
  (adjustable for gauge) or knife (carefully). Strip enough for connection but
  not excessive (keeps joint mechanically strong).

XT30 / XT60 (XT60 Already Defined)
  High-current connector series:
  - XT30: 30A rated, smaller/lighter (good for UBEC power input)
  - XT60: 60A rated, standard for 3S-6S batteries (our main battery connector)
  - XT90: 90A rated, heavy duty (overkill for our project)


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
   SECTION 9: TROUBLESHOOTING TERMS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

COLD SOLDER JOINT
  Poor solder connection that looks dull/grainy instead of shiny. Caused by moving
  joint before solder cools, or insufficient heat. Results in intermittent connection.
  Fix: Reheat with soldering iron until shiny and smooth.

GROUND LOOP
  Unwanted current flow between multiple ground paths at different voltages.
  Causes noise, erratic behavior, flickering OSD. Prevention: Star grounding
  topology (all grounds to ONE point).

INTERMITTENT CONNECTION
  Electrical connection that works sometimes but not always. Causes:
  - Cold solder joint
  - Loose connector
  - Broken wire strand (partially broken)
  - Corrosion
  Troubleshooting: Wiggle wires while powered to reproduce problem.

MAGIC SMOKE
  Humorous term for electronics component failure. "I let the magic smoke out =
  I broke it." Expensive lesson - always double-check polarity and connections!

NO GPS FIX / NO GPS LOCK
  GPS module cannot determine position. Troubleshooting:
  - Verify clear sky view (no roof, no dense trees)
  - Check antenna not damaged/disconnected
  - Verify TX/RX wires not swapped
  - Wait longer (cold start can take 5+ minutes)
  - Check baud rate matches FC configuration

OSCILLATION
  Unstable rapid shaking or vibration. In flight: PID values too high (reduce P
  and D gains). In servo: Mechanical binding or damaged feedback potentiometer.

POWER CYCLING
  Turning device off and on again. Basic troubleshooting step that clears transient
  errors, resets firmware. For our system: Disconnect battery, wait 5 seconds,
  reconnect.

REVERSED SERVO
  Servo moves opposite direction from command (up command = down movement). Fix
  in FC configurator: Enable "reverse" checkbox for that channel. Do NOT physically
  reverse servo wires (changes null position).

RX LOSS / LINK LOST
  Radio receiver not receiving transmitter signal. Causes:
  - Transmitter off or too far away
  - Receiver antenna damaged
  - Frequency interference
  - Low transmission power
  Result: Failsafe activates. Check RSSI (signal strength) telemetry.

SHORT CIRCUIT (Already Defined)
  See Section 1 - Direct connection between + and -, bypassing load.

VOLTAGE SPIKE
  Brief surge in voltage above normal level. Caused by inductive kickback (motor
  stopping), switching transients. Can damage sensitive electronics. Mitigation:
  Capacitors across power supply, TVS diodes on signal lines.

   APPENDIX: COMMON FORMULAS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

OHM'S LAW:
  V = I × R    (Voltage = Current × Resistance)
  I = V / R    (Current = Voltage / Resistance)
  R = V / I    (Resistance = Voltage / Current)

POWER:
  P = V × I         (Power = Voltage × Current)
  P = I² × R        (Power = Current squared × Resistance)
  P = V² / R        (Power = Voltage squared / Resistance)

BATTERY CAPACITY:
  Runtime (hours) = Capacity (mAh) / Current (mA)
  Energy (Wh) = Capacity (Ah) × Voltage (V)

WIRE CURRENT CAPACITY (Approximate):
  12 AWG: 20-25A
  14 AWG: 15-20A
  16 AWG: 10-15A
  18 AWG: 7-10A
  20 AWG: 5-7A
  22 AWG: 3-5A
  24 AWG: 2-3A
  26 AWG: 1-2A (signal wires only)

SERVO TRAVEL:
  Travel (degrees) = (Pulse Width - 1.5ms) × 90° per 0.5ms
  Center: 1.5ms = 0°
  Min: 1.0ms = -90°
  Max: 2.0ms = +90°

LIPO CELL VOLTAGES:
  Full charge:    4.20V per cell (16.8V for 4S)
  Nominal:        3.70V per cell (14.8V for 4S)
  Storage:        3.80V per cell (15.2V for 4S)
  Low warning:    3.50V per cell (14.0V for 4S)
  Critical min:   3.30V per cell (13.2V for 4S)
  Damaged:        <3.00V per cell (DO NOT USE!)
